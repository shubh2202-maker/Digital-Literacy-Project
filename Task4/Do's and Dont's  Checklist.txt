Social Media Do’s:

* Maintain a professional profile
* Use strong passwords
* Think before posting
* Verify information before sharing
* Respect others online

Social Media Don’ts:

* Don’t share personal information publicly
* Don’t spread fake news
* Don’t use offensive language
* Don’t click unknown links
* Don’t overshare private content

Poor digital communication can create misunderstandings and negatively affect professional relationships. For example, sending an email without a proper subject line or greeting may appear rude or unprofessional to the recipient. This can lead to confusion or even missed opportunities in academic or professional settings.

Such situations can be avoided by following proper email etiquette. A well-structured email with a clear subject line, polite tone, and proper sign-off ensures effective communication. It is also important to proofread messages before sending them.

Overall, good digital communication helps in building trust, maintaining professionalism, and avoiding unnecessary problems.
